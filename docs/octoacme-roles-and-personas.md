# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA Testers

### Role Summary
QA Testers validate that features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards. They design test plans, execute tests, and report issues to ensure a high-quality user experience.

### Responsibilities
- Design and execute test plans for new features and regression
- Validate acceptance criteria and document test results
- Identify, document, and track defects
- Collaborate with developers on bug resolution
- Participate in release readiness reviews

### Goals
- Ensure features meet quality standards before release
- Reduce production defects and customer-reported issues
- Maintain comprehensive test coverage

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups to report testing progress and blockers
- Defect reports and test summaries
- Release readiness sign-off

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Clarify acceptance criteria and feature requirements
- **Developers**: Collaborate on defect triage and reproduction steps
- **Project Managers**: Report testing status and release blockers
- **Release Manager**: Provide go/no-go recommendations for releases

---

## Stakeholders

### Role Summary
Stakeholders provide inputs, approvals, and funding for projects. They represent business units, customers, or leadership who have vested interest in project outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define business requirements and success metrics
- Approve scope, timelines, and resource allocation
- Review progress and provide feedback
- Make escalation decisions when needed

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver business value and ROI
- Maintain alignment with organizational priorities
- Support teams with necessary resources and authority

### Typical Communication
- Monthly status reviews and stakeholder briefings
- Approval gates for major milestones
- Ad-hoc escalation communications

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Receive status updates and escalate critical decisions
- **Product Managers**: Align on priorities and success metrics
- **All roles**: Provide guidance and remove organizational blockers

---

## UX Designers

### Role Summary
UX Designers create user interfaces and experiences that are intuitive, accessible, and aligned with user needs. They conduct research, design wireframes and prototypes, and ensure usability standards are met.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing
- Design wireframes, mockups, and interactive prototypes
- Create and maintain design systems and style guides
- Validate designs through user feedback and testing
- Ensure accessibility and usability standards are met

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, user-centered designs
- Maintain consistency across product experiences
- Reduce user friction and improve satisfaction

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- User research findings and insights
- Design handoff documentation and specifications

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Product Managers**: Collaborate on feature requirements and user stories, clarify acceptance criteria
- **Developers**: Provide design specifications and implementation guidance, review built features for design fidelity
- **QA Testers**: Partner on usability testing and validate user experience quality
- **Technical Writers**: Share user flows and terminology for documentation consistency

---

## Technical Writers

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain documentation that helps users, developers, and stakeholders understand products, processes, and systems. They transform complex technical information into clear, accessible content.

### Responsibilities
- Write user guides, API documentation, and onboarding materials
- Document processes, workflows, and best practices
- Create and maintain release notes and changelog
- Collaborate with teams to ensure documentation accuracy
- Maintain documentation standards and style guides

### Goals
- Provide clear, accurate, and accessible documentation
- Reduce support burden through self-service content
- Ensure documentation stays current with product changes

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews and feedback sessions
- Content planning and publication schedules
- Release notes and announcement drafts

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Project Managers**: Gather project context and process documentation needs
- **Developers**: Obtain technical details and review accuracy of documentation
- **Product Managers**: Understand feature context and user scenarios
- **Support Engineers**: Identify common issues and documentation gaps
- **Stakeholders**: Publish release notes and user-facing announcements

---

## Release Managers

### Role Summary
Release Managers own the end-to-end release process, ensuring features are deployed safely, reliably, and on schedule. They coordinate deployments, manage release timelines, and execute rollback procedures when needed.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release schedules and deployment windows
- Ensure pre-release checklists and quality gates are met
- Execute deployments and monitor for issues
- Manage rollback and incident response procedures
- Maintain release documentation and post-deployment reports

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, low-risk releases to production
- Minimize deployment-related incidents
- Maintain predictable release cadence

### Typical Communication
- Release planning meetings and timeline coordination
- Deployment notifications and status updates
- Post-release reports and retrospectives

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Coordinate merge timelines and deployment readiness
- **QA Testers**: Validate release quality and obtain sign-off
- **Product Managers**: Align release scope and timing with roadmap priorities
- **Project Managers**: Report release status and coordinate cross-team dependencies
- **Support Engineers**: Hand off release information and coordinate post-release monitoring
- **Security Lead**: Ensure security reviews are complete before release

---

## Support Engineers

### Role Summary
Support Engineers handle post-release support, triage user-reported issues, and maintain knowledge base content. They serve as the front line for customer issues and escalate technical problems to development teams.

### Responsibilities
- Triage and respond to user-reported issues
- Maintain and update knowledge base articles
- Escalate bugs and technical issues to development teams
- Provide feedback on product usability and common pain points
- Participate in incident response and resolution

### Goals
- Resolve customer issues quickly and effectively
- Reduce repeat issues through documentation and product improvements
- Maintain high customer satisfaction

### Typical Communication
- Ticket updates and customer communications
- Escalation reports and bug submissions
- Knowledge base updates and FAQ maintenance

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Release Manager**: Receive release hand-off and monitor for post-deployment issues
- **Developers**: Escalate technical issues and collaborate on troubleshooting
- **Technical Writers**: Identify documentation gaps and suggest improvements
- **Product Managers**: Provide user feedback and insights on product pain points
- **Stakeholders**: Communicate status of major incidents and resolutions

---

## Security Leads

### Role Summary
Security Leads advise on secure development practices, conduct security reviews, and manage security incident response. They ensure products and processes meet security standards and compliance requirements.

### Responsibilities
- Define and promote secure development best practices
- Review code, architecture, and releases for security vulnerabilities
- Conduct security assessments and penetration testing
- Manage security incident response and remediation
- Ensure compliance with security policies and regulations

### Goals
- Minimize security vulnerabilities in products and systems
- Ensure rapid response to security incidents
- Maintain compliance with security standards

### Typical Communication
- Security review findings and recommendations
- Incident response communications and post-mortems
- Training sessions and security best practice guidance

### Interactions with Other Roles
- **Developers**: Provide security training, review code for vulnerabilities, and guide remediation efforts
- **Product Managers**: Review feature designs for security implications and compliance needs
- **Project Managers**: Report security risks and coordinate remediation timelines
- **Release Managers**: Conduct pre-release security reviews and approve security readiness
- **Stakeholders**: Communicate security posture, compliance status, and incident resolutions
